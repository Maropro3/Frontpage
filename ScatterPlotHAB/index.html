The search for habitable worlds


One of the main reasons to be fascinated with the discoveries of exoplanets is the possibility of finding other habitable worlds like Earth and possibly
extraterrestrial life. In the Sankey diagram below you can observe the confirmed exoplanets and the relationships between different classifications.








Finding possible habitable exoplanets is a complicated task. With the few data and exoplanets parameters, having some approximation to how closely
the exoplanet in question resembles Earth is a good heuristic to determine its habitability. In the chart below, the Earth Similarity Index (ESI)
and Cobb-Douglas Habitability Score (CDHS) are plotted for the exoplanets with enough data to compute them.
